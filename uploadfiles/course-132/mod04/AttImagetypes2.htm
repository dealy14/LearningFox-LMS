In this particular case the proximity of the "R" (in the molded in "RGV") to the adjacent bead in the tank (in the close-up view) tells the manufacturer on which build station this tank was assembled. A tank built on a different station had a clear 1/2" gap between this cited bead and the "R" marking. This is clearly not something that one might recognize as a significant difference, but in this case it is.

The importance of the MACRO view is that the marking (whether it's a paint marking, hand writing, or a dot) is likely to be more helpful if the location of the identified mark is also made clear. This MACRO view is also helpful to another technician or engineer when attempting to identify a similar problem whether comparing markings, or some other significant feature.
