Answer 4: Password Protected Screensavers

Either Jan could have logged off her computer completely, or she could have locked down her workstation with a password-protected screensaver so that no one could access her e-mail account. Password-protected screen savers help ensure confidentiality of sensitive data that resides on workstations and/or servers, and are required by USPTO policy. This reduces the possibility of unauthorized users gaining access to sensitive data by simply sitting down at an unattended computer.

If you are on Windows XP, your workstation should be automatically set to turn on the password-protected screensaver after 15 minutes of inactivity. Your password will be your workstation password.
