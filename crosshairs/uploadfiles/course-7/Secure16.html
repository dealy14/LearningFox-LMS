Sensitive Information

The USPTO mission is to protect rights to inventions through issuance of patents and the commercialization of new products and services through registration of trademarks. In doing this, the USPTO must be able to recognize and protect sensitive information since this is a requirement of several Federal laws including the Patent Act and the Privacy Act. 

Sensitive information requires protection due to the risk and magnitude of loss or harm that could result from the unauthorized use, loss, alteration, or disclosure of the information. Examples include:

  • Information whose loss, or improper use or disclosure, could adversely affect the USPTO or other federal agency missions

  • Proprietary and procurement information

  • Records about individuals that are protected under the Privacy Act

  • Information not releasable under the Freedom of Information Act

  • Financial data
