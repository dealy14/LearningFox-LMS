Countermeasure: Password Hints and Tips

Protect yourself and the USPTO by doing the following:

  • Do not share individual passwords with anyone.

  • If you suspect your password has been compromised, change your password immediately.

  • Do not set applications to remember your password the next time you visit the application.

  • Do not use any part of a name or a word in any language in your password.

  • Do not use the same character more than three times in a row (e.g. 'AAAAAAA1' is not acceptable, but 'A%rmp2g3' and 'A%AdmA2g3' are acceptable).

  • Change your password every 90 days. (The system will prompt you.)

  • Use strategies such as substitution to make a strong password that you can remember (e.g. 5 for S and 0 for O in the example wRk@u5pt0.).

  • Do not use your PTO passwords for any account passwords on systems outside of PTO. Use different passwords for different accounts you might use on the Internet.
